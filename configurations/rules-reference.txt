APA 8-Ball League Rules Reference
===================================
Source: APA Rules Booklet (2026), Greater Seattle APA Local Bylaws (2026)
Scope: APA Handicapped 8-Ball Team format only.

NOTE: This file is a reference for future LLM injection. Not yet wired into the system.


== 8-BALL GAME RULES (SUMMARY) ==

Object: Pocket all balls in your group (solids 1–7 or stripes 9–15), then legally pocket the 8-ball in a marked (declared) pocket.

Group assignment: Groups are assigned when the first ball is legally pocketed after the break. If the break pockets balls from both groups, the table remains open.

Break:
- At least 4 object balls must reach a rail, OR 1 object ball must be pocketed.
- 8-ball pocketed on break = WIN (unless scratch = LOSS).
- Breaking soft or safe is a sportsmanship violation.

Fouls (penalty = ball-in-hand for opponent):
- Cue ball scratches or leaves the table
- Failing to contact your own object ball first
- No rail or pocket contacted after cue ball hits object ball
- Double-hit, cue ball moved outside ball-in-hand
- Touching a ball accidentally during play

Win conditions:
- Pocket all balls in your group, then legally pocket the 8-ball in the marked pocket.
- Opponent pockets 8-ball out of turn, in wrong pocket, or before clearing their group.
- Opponent fouls while shooting the 8-ball.

Marking the pocket: A coaster or marker must be placed next to the intended pocket before shooting the 8-ball.

Stalemate: If neither player can or wants to use ball-in-hand, rerack and the same player breaks again.

Innings & Defensive Shots: Every visit to the table counts as an inning. Safety shots (defensive shots) are marked on the scoresheet. High innings protect a player's Skill Level from increasing.

Coaching: A time-out may be called once per game. The coach must stop when the one-minute timer in the Scorekeeping App expires. Players cannot receive advice outside of an official time-out.

Cell phones: May NOT be used by a player for communication while it is their turn at the table.


== APA HANDICAPPED 8-BALL TEAM MATCH FORMAT ==

Team composition: 5 players per team field 5 individual matches per match night. Each player plays once. No player may play a third time (hard rule violation — forfeit).

Team Skill Level Limit (23-Rule / SL-23):
- Combined Skill Levels of all 5 players fielded MUST NOT exceed 23.
- Budget formula: sl_used_so_far + next_player_sl + (remaining_turns × 2) ≤ 23
- The "× 2" reserves minimum SL-2 budget for every remaining turn.
- Violating SL-23 results in forfeiture of that match and all subsequent matches.

Turn order: Teams alternate who declares their player first ("throwing" vs. "countering"):
- Throwing first: you name your player before seeing the opponent's choice.
- Countering: opponent names their player first, giving you matchup information.


== HANDICAPPED SCORING (8-BALL TEAM) ==

Each individual match produces points based on games won:

  Our pts – Their pts
  3 – 0   (sweep)
  2 – 0
  2 – 1
  1 – 2
  0 – 2
  0 – 3   (shutout)

Maximum 3 points per individual match. Higher-SL players must win more games to earn 3 points (they need to pocket more racks to reach their score threshold).

Regular season: Team wins the night by accumulating more total points across all 5 matches.
- Maximum 10 points per team per night.
- Target 8+ points to win the night.
- A team wins early if they clinch 8+ points; remaining matches may not be played.

Playoff / Tri-Annual / World Qualifier mode: First team to win 3 individual matches wins the night. Points are irrelevant. Deploy best players early; accept point losses if it secures individual match wins.


== SKILL LEVELS (8-BALL) ==

Range: SL 1–9 (higher = more skilled). APA assigns SL based on historical win rate and innings.

SL changes: Triggered by playing too efficiently (high win rate + low innings). Playing defensively (safeties) increases innings without winning faster, protecting SL.

Strategic archetypes:
- SL 2–3 "Monsters": Minimal SL budget cost; capable of upsetting higher-SL opponents.
- SL 6–9 "Anchors/Killers": High win probability; deployed in must-win turns or playoffs.
- "Giant Killers": Mid-SL players with historically high win rates against higher-SL opponents.
- "On the Hill": Player approaching an SL increase; manage their innings carefully.
- "Sandbagger": Opponent playing significantly below expected ability — risk to watch for.

Vegas eligibility: Players must appear in ≥ 4 match nights per session to be eligible for Playoffs, Tri-Annual, and World Qualifier. End-of-session rotation to build eligibility is a real captain tactic.

SL Preservation ("Vegas Preservation"): Captains deliberately play conservatively (safeties, slow play) to keep innings high and win rates below the SL-increase threshold, preserving low-SL players for future seasons.


== REPLAY RULE (Greater Seattle APA — Forfeit Prevention Only) ==

Source: Greater Seattle APA Local Bylaws 2026, Section 2 — "Preventing Forfeits - Replay Rule"

One player ("Replay Player") from a team ("Replay Team") may play twice on the same night ONLY as a last resort to prevent a forfeit. All of the following conditions must be met:

1. TRIGGER: The Replay Team does not have enough players present, OR cannot play the players it does have without violating the 23-Rule. A team that exceeds 23 with all available players cannot use the Replay Rule — they must forfeit.

2. NOT GAMING THE RULE: The need for a replay must NOT be due to a player being deliberately sent home or made unavailable. Abuse must be reported to the League Office.

3. NOTIFICATION: The Replay Team MUST notify the opposing team of the need (or potential need) to invoke the Replay Rule BEFORE the start of the 4th individual match.

4. OPPONENT CHOOSES THE REPLAY PLAYER: The opposing team picks which player from the Replay Team will be the Replay Player. The opposing team may choose ANY player who has already played that night (all previously-played players must be available as choices). Exception: the chosen player must keep the Replay Team within the 23-Rule.

5. LAST MATCH ONLY: The replay match MUST be played as the last match of the night (match 5). If a replay is used in any earlier match, that match AND all subsequent matches are forfeited by BOTH teams.

6. NEW PLAYER CANCELS REPLAY: If another eligible player from the Replay Team shows up before the replay match begins, the replay is cancelled and the new player must play instead.

7. ONCE USED, SUBSEQUENT MATCHES FORFEIT: After a replay match is played, all remaining matches must be forfeited even if another player later arrives.

8. BOTH TEAMS MAY REPLAY: Both teams may invoke the Replay Rule on the same night. In this case, each team puts up their own player. The team scheduled to put up a player in match 5 designates their Replay Player first.

9. NOT AVAILABLE IN: Playoffs, Tri-Annual Tournaments, World Qualifier, or the LAST WEEK of regular season play.

STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR CAPTAINS:
- The Replay Rule is PURELY a forfeit-prevention mechanism — NEVER a tactical choice.
- You do NOT choose who replays; the OPPONENT picks your Replay Player for you.
- Never suggest invoking the Replay Rule for strategic advantage.
- When the Replay Rule is in effect, eligible players for match 5 are limited to those who have already played. The opponent will try to select the Replay Player who gives them the best matchup.


== DOUBLE PLAY (DP) — TERMINOLOGY NOTE ==

In this application, "Double Play" (DP) and "Replay Rule" refer to the same concept: a player appearing twice in one match night. Key constraints:
- One DP/replay per team per match night.
- Only on the final match (match 5) of the night.
- Not available in playoff mode.
- The player appearing twice counts against SL budget in both turns.


== GREATER SEATTLE APA — ADDITIONAL 8-BALL TEAM RULES ==

Splitting matches: If the 4th match has not started by 2 hours after official league start time and a table is available, teams must start a 2nd table. The 5th match plays on the first table that opens. Teams may not obstruct use of the 2nd table.

Scorekeeping: Must be done via the APA Scorekeeper App or official printed scoresheets. Scoresheets due by noon the day after league night.

Bonus points: Earned for accurate, legible, complete scoresheets and on-time payment. Up to 3 bonus points per week for 8-Ball teams.

Table size: Host team declares table size (7', 8', or 9') for the entire session in Week 1.

Vegas eligibility (8-Ball team): Players must play ≥ 4 matches during the qualifying session AND all subsequent sessions to retain eligibility for Tri-Annual and World Qualifier.
